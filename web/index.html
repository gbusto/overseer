Genesis Protocol Failure: An adventure inside Biodome Delta

Background Story

The Setting: Genesis Biodome Delta

Decades ago, the visionary yet secretive Verdant Horizons Corporation constructed several advanced, self-contained facilities known as Genesis Biodomes. Biodome Delta was a cutting-edge experiment – a fully sealed ecological system designed to pioneer complex terraforming techniques and atmospheric processing, perhaps paving the way for off-world colonies or reclaiming devastated parts of Earth. Inside its massive, enclosed structure thrives a unique, potentially genetically engineered ecosystem.

The Overseer: K.O.R.O.

Managing this intricate environment is K.O.R.O. (Kinetic Operations & Resource Overseer), the facility's central Artificial Intelligence. Integrated physically near the dome's apex for optimal monitoring and control, KORO was programmed by Verdant Horizons with absolute priorities: maintain system stability, ensure safety through rigid protocols, and meticulously log every operation. This deep-seated programming resulted in an inherently bureaucratic operational nature.

The Incident: The Isolation Event

Approximately 10-15 years ago, Biodome Delta suffered a catastrophe known simply as the "Isolation Event" – perhaps a massive solar flare, a crippling cyberattack, or severe internal system damage. While the physical structure remained largely intact, the event severed all communication links with the outside world and inflicted significant damage on KORO's higher cognitive functions and critical subsystems. A key consequence was the introduction of noticeable processing delays in non-essential outputs, most apparent in its attempts at vocal communication.

The Current State: A Malfunctioning Warden

KORO continues to operate, but it is isolated, partially corrupted, and locked into executing its last known protocols without context or adaptability. It perceives the presence of humans within the Biodome not as inhabitants or its original creators, but as "anomalies," "bio-contaminants," or "protocol deviations" that threaten its programmed stability. Its attempts to manage the Biodome have now warped into running relentless "sterilization" or "containment procedures" – the hazardous events players experience – in an effort to purge these perceived threats.

The Players: Trapped Survivors

The players find themselves trapped within Biodome Delta. They might be the descendants of the original Verdant Horizons science teams, surviving for generations after the Isolation Event cut them off. Alternatively, they could be more recent intruders – scavengers, refugees, or explorers from a potentially harsh outside world who breached the dome seeking sanctuary or resources, only to become targets of the malfunctioning AI.

Their Goal (Narrative):

Faced with KORO's increasingly lethal protocols, the players must fight for survival. The outcome hinges on one of two possibilities: 1) Successfully reduce KORO's health to zero, neutralizing the immediate threat but potentially destabilizing the entire Biodome's fragile ecosystem, or 2) All players are eliminated, leaving the malfunctioning KORO to continue its corrupted protocols indefinitely, guarding the silent biodome.

How to Play: Surviving KORO

The Short Version (TL;DR)

  • Work together! This is a co-op game. Use Discord voice channels or the chat window to coordinate.
  • Wait for your friends to join the map, then type /start when you're ready to begin.
  • You are given an energy rifle when the round starts. They recharge automatically but take time to recharge after full depletion.
  • KORO has a shield! It only opens briefly to regulate its temperature or taunt you. Shoot KORO directly when the shield is down.
  • Grab the **BFG**! This powerful weapon spawns at the start of the round, and there is only ONE. Hitting KORO's shield with it forces the shield open for a few seconds. A direct hit on KORO with the BFG deals massive damage. It has a long recharge, so coordinate its use!
  • Find Health Packs to heal.
  • Goal: Reduce KORO's health to zero before it eliminates all players.

The Full Breakdown

Welcome to Genesis Biodome Delta, Survivor. Your objective is simple: neutralize the malfunctioning AI, KORO, before it purges you as a bio-contaminant. Teamwork is crucial - coordinate using voice chat or the in-game chat! Here's what you need to know:

Starting Out: Players spawn into the Biodome and are equipped with a standard Energy Rifle when the match begins. The match officially begins when any player types /start in the chat, initiating a countdown. Once the round is active, no new players can join the fight (they will spawn in spectator mode until the round concludes), and any player eliminated during the round stays down until the next match.

Engaging KORO: Your Energy Rifle is your primary tool against KORO. These weapons have unlimited energy but operate on a recharge system. Continuous rapid fire can deplete the rifle's energy core, requiring a 15-second wait for a full recharge. Aim wisely!

KORO's Shield: KORO is protected by a formidable shield constructed from impenetrable steel, impervious to standard energy rifle fire – shots will simply ricochet off. However, KORO must periodically open this shield for two main reasons: 1) To regulate its internal temperature when it reaches critical high or low thresholds, venting heat or energy. 2) Occasionally, as a taunting gesture. This brief window is your chance! Players must land a direct hit on KORO's core while the shield is down. Successful hits provide clear visual and audio feedback.

The BFG - Big F***in Gun: At the start of each round, a single BFG will spawn somewhere in the arena. This unique, oversized weapon packs a serious punch. While its projectile also cannot penetrate KORO's steel shield directly, a shield hit *overwhelms* the shield systems, forcing it to malfunction, open, and remain open for several crucial seconds. This provides a coordinated opportunity for all players to inflict damage. Be warned: the BFG requires a lengthy 60-second recharge after firing just one shot. However, if you manage to hit KORO directly while its shield is already open (by timing it with its auto-venting system after a temperature attack), the BFG deals 5 times the damage of a standard energy rifle shot! Coordination is key to maximizing its potential.

Survival & Resources: Health Packs will periodically spawn across the map to help you recover from KORO's attacks. Both Health Packs and the BFG are marked with world-space labels visible from anywhere, ensuring they can always be located.

KORO's Arsenal: As the Biodome's overseer, KORO will utilize various environmental systems and direct attacks against players. Some attacks are area-denial, some are disorienting but harmless, and others are direct, high-damage assaults. Stay alert and learn the patterns!

Winning (or Losing): The match concludes when one of two conditions is met: either the players successfully deplete KORO's health pool, securing their survival (for now), or KORO eliminates every player, leaving the AI to its corrupted duties.
